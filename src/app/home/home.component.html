Earth is the third planet in our system. For all people it is a unique place to live, but for the universe it is only one small point. Our star is an average sphere of gas, which is full in our galaxy. The Earth is the only planet in the Solar System that is covered by water in a liquid state. As much as 71% of our planet's surface are oceans and seas, which are subject to high tides and outflows caused by our natural satellite - the Moon, and the Sun. It has flattened poles, which is why it is not a sphere and so-called geoid.

The magnetic field produced by the Earth protects us through lethal solar and cosmic rays. The rotating planet provide by light and darkness, day and night. The earth is surrounded by a gas coat called the atmosphere. It reaches theoretically up to 35 thousand kilometers, but practically ends at 4,000 kilometers. It is divided into five layers, successively called troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere, ionosphere and exosphere. In the troposphere, which accounts for 80% of the entire atmosphere, we observe a temperature drop with an increase in height by an average of 6oC per 1 km. The stratosphere has a constant temperature of -55 ° C, while in the mesosphere the temperature initially rises to 0 ° C, then it decreases to -68 ° C. On the contrary, it happens on the surface of the planet. Here, as the depth increases, the temperature rises by an average of 3oC per 100m.
